Mother Monster

The first four or five or six times I encountered Lady Gaga, in London or Paris or
New York, backstage in Vegas or Madison Square Garden or the O2 arena,
at the top of the Skytree in Tokyo or from inside a giant replica of her fragrance bottle at
a party at the Guggenheim, or even when, six years ago, we hung out in her kitchen in Malibu and
danced and cried while listening to music—“Like, real Italian style,” she said—every single one of
those times, in all of those places, she was both there and not there.
She was viscerally present and accounted for but also somehow absent. This is not a complaint.
